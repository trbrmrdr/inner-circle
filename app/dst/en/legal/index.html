Logs & Cookies.

Web server logs.

When using the website, anonymized access data (e.g. log files, IP address, date and time of access, name of the accessed file, access status, page from which access was made, top-level domain, used web browser, used operating system) are stored. We use this data for statistical purposes, for technical evaluations, and to determine access frequencies in order to improve user-friendliness and functionality.

Cookies.

The website uses so-called cookies, which are small text files that are stored on your device with the help of your browser. They do not cause any damage, but serve to facilitate your use of the website and make it more individual.

If you do not wish to accept cookies, you can set up your browser to inform you about the setting of cookies and to allow this only in individual cases. In addition, common browsers have the option to not allow or disable cookies. You can usually find instructions on how to manage cookies in your browser's help function or in the operating manual of your device. For common browsers, you can also access instructions on how to disable cookies at the following links:

  • Internet Explorer 
  • Firefox
  • Chrome
  • Safari
  • Opera

Some cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. The technically necessary cookies remain stored on your device for a certain period of time or until deleted. You can delete technically necessary cookies from your browser after using the website.

Visitors to the website are informed about the collection of personal data on the corresponding pages. We use personal data for the processing of which you have given us your consent (e.g. ordering newsletters or brochures), or which is necessary for the performance of a contract or in the course of initiating business.

Personal data that we collect via the website will only be used in accordance with the purpose arising from the page in question and will be stored until this purpose has been fulfilled. In the event of a contract being concluded, the data from the contractual relationship will be stored until the expiry of the statutory retention or limitation periods.

The data collected on the website is forwarded internally when you enter it. The confidentiality of the data is maintained. The data is only passed on to third parties if this is necessary (e.g. dispatch of newsletters or brochures by service providers).

By using state-of-the-art security software and certified coding and encryption procedures, our IT infrastructure complies with international security standards. In addition, we have taken additional, comprehensive security precautions and technical and organisational measures to protect your data from loss, unauthorised access or misuse in accordance with the state of the art for Internet access to accounts and deposits.

Notwithstanding the measures taken to protect your data, data protection and confidentiality may be limited when processing data via universally accessible media. We recommend that particularly sensitive data, especially personal data and data relating to customer relationships (account, deposit, transactions, etc.), should only be transmitted via secure communication channels. Your rights.

With regard to your personal data, you have the following data subject rights (Art. 15 to 21 DSGVO):

  • Right to information:
    You can request information about whether and to what extent we process personal data about you.
  • Right to rectification, erasure and restriction of processing:
    You can have inaccurate or incomplete personal data concerning you corrected or request that your data be deleted (e.g. if it is no longer necessary, you have withdrawn your consent or the data processing is unlawful) or the processing of your data be restricted.
  • Right of withdrawal:
    You can revoke your consent to the processing of your data at any time. The revocation of consent is only effective for the future and does not affect the lawfulness of the data processed until the revocation. The revocation also has no influence on data processing on the basis of another legal basis.
  • Right to data portability:
    You can have your data that you have provided to us transferred in a structured, common and machine-readable format.
  • Right of appeal:
    If you feel that your rights have been violated by our data processing, you can lodge a complaint with the Liechtenstein data protection authority or another supervisory authority in an EU or EEA member state (e.g. at the place of residence or place of work or at the place of the breach of the GDPR).
